Project Description

The Clean Cities Uganda project is a digital application developed by a group of a group youth climate activists from Uganda. The major rationale behind the application development is to engage users in the effort to clean up Uganda's cities. The app allows users to easily order garbage and plastic waste pick up from the comfort of their own homes.

The app operates on a subscription model, where users pay a small monthly fee to access the service. In addition to making it easy for users to dispose of their waste responsibly, the Clean Cities Uganda app also provides an opportunity for users to make some extra money. By selling their plastic waste to the collection company, users can help to reduce the amount of plastic pollution in Uganda's cities while also earning some extra income.

In addition to its practical benefits, the Clean Cities Uganda app is also designed to raise awareness about proper waste management in all 11 of Uganda's cities. Through the app, users can learn about the importance of waste segregation and recycling, and can access tips and resources for reducing their waste and making more environmentally friendly choices.

The Clean Cities Uganda project is currently in an exciting stage of development. We have already developed a prototype of the app and are conducting trials in Lira City. The feedback from these studies is helping us to make improvements to the app and ensure that it is user-friendly and effective. Overall, the response to the Clean Cities Uganda project has been very positive, and we are looking forward to continuing to work with the city council and the people of Lira City to make the project a success. We believe that the Clean Cities Uganda app has the potential to make a real difference in the fight against plastic pollution and waste mismanagement in Uganda, and we are committed to working hard to make that happen.

The future prospects for the Clean Cities Uganda project are ambitious and exciting. One of the main goals of the project is to establish large biogas plants in all 11 of Uganda's cities. These biogas plants will convert organic waste into clean energy, providing a sustainable and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional fossil fuels.

In addition to establishing biogas plants, the Clean Cities Uganda project also aims to partner with plastic recycling companies to promote the recycling of more plastic waste. By working with these companies, the Clean Cities Uganda project hopes to reduce plastic pollution in Uganda and help to create a more sustainable future for the country.

The Clean Cities Uganda app is therefore an important tool in the fight against plastic waste and improper waste management in Uganda. The team behind the app, made up of dedicated and determined youth climate activists, have been working tirelessly to make this project a success. From the very beginning, the members of the team have been making contributions from their own income to see the project through. We are now at a stage where we are ready to move to the implementation phase, but we are limited by funds.

Our Strategies

To ensure the project's success, the team plans to implement awareness campaigns to educate the public on proper waste management practices, engage local communities, partner with local organizations, offer a subsidized service to low-income households, work with local collection and recycling companies, establish biogas plants to convert organic waste into clean energy, partner with plastic recycling companies, continuously monitor and evaluate the app's performance, generate revenue through selling collected waste and/or paid subscriptions, and set measurable goals to regularly monitor progress.

About the App

This project uses a mobile application to promote better waste management in Ugandan cities, while also producing cleaner energy through the production of bio gas and charcoal briquettes. The digital application is also raising awareness about the importance of proper waste management, which is essential in building climate resilience.

Partnership

As part of our efforts to reduce plastic pollution, we are forming partnerships with plastic recycling companies in Uganda to promote the responsible disposal and recycling of plastic waste. Through this partnership, we are working to reduce the environmental impact of plastic pollution and create a cleaner, healthier future for all.
